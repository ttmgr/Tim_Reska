Bundibugyo ebolavirus epidemic

DRC's 17th Ebola outbreak and first caused by Bundibugyo ebolavirus. Centered in Ituri Province with confirmed spread to Goma (1M+ population, rebel-held), Kinshasa (~1,000 km from epicenter), and Uganda. WHO declared a PHEIC on 17 May 2026. No licensed vaccine or therapeutics exist — all approved countermeasures target Zaire ebolavirus only. Rwanda has closed the Rubavu-Goma border. At least 4 healthcare workers dead. 6 Americans exposed, 1 symptomatic. Field hospitals being established in Bunia as facilities are overwhelmed. True scale likely underestimated.

    Bundibugyo ebolavirus is one of six known species in the genus Orthoebolavirus that causes Ebola virus disease in humans. It was first identified during the 2007-2008 outbreak in Bundibugyo District, western Uganda.

    Historical CFR: 25-50%. Incubation: 2-21 days. Transmission: direct contact with blood, secretions, organs, or body fluids of infected people, and with contaminated surfaces and materials.

    No approved medical countermeasures: All licensed Ebola vaccines (rVSV-ZEBOV/Ervebo, Ad26.ZEBOV/MVA-BN-Filo) and therapeutics (mAb114/Ebanga, REGN-EB3/Inmazeb) were developed specifically against Zaire ebolavirus and are not expected to provide protection against Bundibugyo virus.

    Diagnostic challenge: Standard rapid diagnostic tests detect only Zaire ebolavirus. Bundibugyo-specific RT-PCR assays are required, which are typically available only at national reference laboratories.

    WHO — PHEIC declaration, technical team deployment, $500K emergency funding.

    Africa CDC — Continental coordination, IMST activation, Director-General visiting affected countries.

    US CDC — 30+ staff in DRC, additional deployments, lab support, American evacuation coordination.

    MSF — Already operating in Ituri, scaling up medical response.

    DRC Ministry of Health / INRB — National outbreak declaration, laboratory confirmation.

    Uganda Ministry of Health — National response for imported cases.
