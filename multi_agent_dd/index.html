Why This Is Not a ChatGPT Prompt

Single-context role-play ("pretend you are Agent A, now pretend you are Agent B") produces consensus mush. The agents anchor each other, the validator rationalises its own prior reasoning, and critique gets papered over. This architecture prevents all three failure modes.

Structural Guarantee 1

Parallel Isolated Contexts

Phases 1a, 1b, and 1c run simultaneously in separate agent contexts. Agent A cannot see Agent B's reasoning while it works. The first agent's framing cannot anchor the other two.

3 subagentsIsolated
Structural Guarantee 2

Blind Validation

The Phase 3 validator sees only the bundle of specialist and synthesis outputs. It never sees the orchestrator's reasoning. It cannot rationalise prior conclusions because it never had access to them.

Blind contextIndependent
Structural Guarantee 3

Forced Critique Honour

Phase 4 synthesis is required to incorporate every validator revision. On a live demonstration run, this changed the base-case IRR label from "evidence-led" to "cohort-led with entity-specific uncertainty" and the combined-stress scenario from 6.5% to 4–6%.

U-1

Unsourced performance figure

A single press reference with no source URL or access date, used as a stress denominator in Phase 2b. The validator flagged this as an unsupported claim and required the synthesis to treat it as a data-point-of-record, not an analytical anchor.

W-7

Combined stress treated as independent

The "severe" combined stress scenario treated three correlated shocks as independent. The validator identified that the underlying drivers are positively correlated and required a correlation-adjusted recomputation.

CC-2

Commitment size discrepancy

Total commitments reported at two different figures depending on the source (firm-disclosed vs press-reported). Phase 1a silently adopted the higher figure. The validator flagged the discrepancy and required explicit acknowledgment in the synthesis.

R-3

Correlation-adjusted combined stress

Required revision: recompute the combined deal-economics stress with correlation adjustment. Changed the outcome from a single-point estimate to a range reflecting correlated downside. The final synthesis reflects the corrected range.

What the output looks like

  1. Minto-structured governing recommendation Three supporting arguments, each backed by evidence from the specialist agents. Every claim sourced, every assumption labelled, every confidence rating justified.
  2. Staged escalation with testable conditions Ticket-size escalation tied to specific DDQ items the buyer can verify. Not a binary yes/no — a decision ladder with clear gates.
  3. Correlation-adjusted stress scenarios Combined downside scenarios with correlated drivers, not independent additive shocks. The validator enforces this — single-context approaches never catch the correlation error.
